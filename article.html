Computer programming is the process of designing, writing, testing, and maintaining the source code of computer software. It involves the creation of instructions, algorithms, and data structures that enable a computer to perform specific tasks or functions. LITERATURE REVIEW
Virtual learning is a method of education that is facilitated through digital technologies, such as computers, the internet, and mobile devices. It allows for education to be delivered in a remote or online environment, which can provide greater flexibility and access to learning opportunities. Virtual learning can take many forms, including online courses, webinars, video conferencing, and virtual classrooms. These technologies allow students to access learning materials and communicate with teachers and other students from anywhere with an internet connection. Virtual learning has become increasingly popular in recent years, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, as it allows for continued learning despite physical distancing measures. It has also provided opportunities for people who may not have had access to traditional forms of education due to geographic, financial, or other constraints. While virtual learning has its benefits, it also presents challenges, such as the need for reliable internet access, potential distractions at home, and the need for self-motivation and time management skills. However, as technology continues to advance, virtual learning is likely to become an increasingly important part of education. THEORY
The benefits of virtual learning include flexibility, convenience, and accessibility, as learners can fit education into their busy schedules and access content from anywhere at any time. Additionally, virtual learning can be more cost-effective than traditional classroom learning, as there are fewer physical resources required. However, virtual learning also presents some challenges, such as the need for learners to be self-motivated and disciplined, the potential for technological difficulties, and the absence of face-to-face interaction with instructors and classmates. Overall, virtual learning is a growing trend in education that has the potential to expand access to education and transform the way we learn. Connectivism is a learning theory that was developed by George Siemens and Stephen Downes. It suggests that learning is not just about acquiring information or knowledge, but rather about making connections and creating networks between people and resources. According to this theory, learning happens through a process of connecting nodes or information sources, and learners must be able to navigate these networks in order to find relevant and reliable information. Connectivism emphasizes the importance of technology in the learning process, particularly in the form of online networks and social media platforms. It suggests that technology has enabled us to create vast networks of knowledge and information, and that we can use these networks to enhance our learning. The theory also highlights the importance of critical thinking and evaluation skills, as learners must be able to assess the reliability and validity of the information they encounter in these networks. It suggests that the ability to effectively navigate and evaluate information networks is a crucial skill in the digital age. Overall, Connectivism represents a shift from traditional, individual-focused learning theories to a more collaborative and network-based approach to learning.

METHODOLOGY
There have been several studies conducted on the effectiveness of traditional learning versus virtual learning in teaching computer programming. One study conducted by Chen et al. (2019) compared the learning outcomes and attitudes of students towards traditional and online learning approaches in teaching Java programming. The results showed that both approaches were effective in improving students' programming skills and knowledge, but students in the online learning group had better attitudes towards learning and reported higher levels of engagement. Similarly, another study by Al-Ajlan (2019) compared the effectiveness of traditional classroom instruction and online learning in teaching programming concepts. The results showed that online learning was more effective in enhancing students' understanding of programming concepts, as students were able to learn at their own pace and had access to a wider range of learning resources. However, some studies have also reported that traditional learning may have some advantages over virtual learning in teaching computer programming. For example, a study by Eman et al. (2021) found that traditional classroom instruction was more effective in promoting students' critical thinking skills and problem-solving abilities than online learning. Overall, while there is some evidence to suggest that virtual learning can be effective in teaching computer programming, further research is needed to fully understand the advantages and disadvantages of traditional and virtual learning approaches in this field. It is important to consider factors such as student learning preferences, access to technology and resources, and the specific learning outcomes being targeted when designing and implementing computer programming courses.   Traditional learning refers to the conventional approach to education where students attend classes in a physical classroom, interact with teachers and peers, and learn through lectures, discussions, and hands-on activities. This approach has been widely used for centuries and is still the dominant mode of education in many parts of the world. Research has shown that traditional learning has several advantages over other forms of education. For example, traditional learning allows for face-to-face interaction between teachers and students, which can help to build strong relationships and enhance learning outcomes (Kuh, 2009). In addition, traditional learning provides students with a structured and disciplined learning environment, which can help to promote focus and concentration (Lipnevich et al., 2011). *In summary, traditional learning has several advantages, including opportunities for face-to-face interaction, a structured learning environment, opportunities for social interaction and collaboration, and a sense of community and belonging. These advantages may contribute to better learning outcomes and a more positive learning experience for students.

DATA ANALYSIS
Cognitive learning theory emphasizes the active mental processes involved in learning, such as attention, memory, and problem-solving. It is relevant to the study of both traditional and virtual learning approaches in computer programming education. A comparative study by Wu and Wu (2021) examined the effects of traditional face-to-face instruction and virtual instruction on the cognitive load and learning outcomes of students in a computer programming course. The authors found that virtual instruction resulted in higher cognitive load, but also higher learning outcomes, indicating that virtual instruction may be more effective in promoting deep learning and problem-solving skills. Another study by Al-Emran et al. (2019) investigated the impact of cognitive load theory on learning outcomes in a virtual learning environment for computer programming. The authors found that by reducing cognitive load through multimedia design and instructional support, students were able to achieve higher learning outcomes. Overall, cognitive learning theory suggests that the design of instructional materials and delivery methods should be carefully tailored to support the cognitive processes involved in learning computer programming, whether through traditional or virtual approaches.
